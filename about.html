Professional Background

Nearly Two Decades in Construction & Manufacturing

Daniel J. Cullen brings close to twenty years of hands-on experience in general construction operations and strategic business management to his role as Director at Precision Metal Fab Inc. in Delafield, Wisconsin. He holds a Bachelor of Science in Construction Management and has spent his career learning what it takes to build organizations that last — from the ground up.

Since joining Precision Metal Fab, Daniel has driven strategic planning, sales growth, and major capital investments that have strengthened the company's competitive position in the miscellaneous metals market. His approach blends operational discipline with a genuine belief in people: "Find good people, get them what they need, and get out of their way." You can follow his professional thinking on his Substack newsletter, where he writes about leadership, industry, and community.

Leadership Philosophy

People First, Results Follow

For Cullen, operational excellence begins with the team. His leadership style is direct and collaborative — he sets high standards and then invests in the people who meet them. At Precision Metal Fab, this has meant a focused commitment to talent recruitment, employee development, and ensuring workers feel both heard and equipped to grow.

That philosophy has translated into measurable outcomes: a stronger client base, improved efficiency, and a culture that attracts the kind of skilled tradespeople Wisconsin manufacturing depends on. Daniel regularly shares insights on his professional profile on LinkedIn and speaks on workforce development topics at Waukesha County Technical College. His piece on building a people-first manufacturing culture explores this approach in depth.

Community & Service

Coaching, Faith, and Giving Back

Daniel's commitment to community is as consistent as his work in the boardroom. He is a boxing instructor with Rock Steady Boxing in Janesville — a program that uses non-contact boxing training to help individuals with Parkinson's disease improve their quality of life — and a coach for the Waukesha South Blackshirts youth basketball program. He is also a catechist and Family Program organizer at St. Anthony's on the Lake in Delafield. His article on what coaching teaches about leadership captures the lessons he's drawn from both the gym and the court.

As a program member and speaker with The Tim Cullen Institute, Daniel contributes to civic engagement and leadership development across Wisconsin. He has also presented at Waukesha County Technical College, sharing the practical lessons of manufacturing leadership with the next generation of tradespeople and managers. You can find more of his public writing and commentary at his Substack page.

Writing & Publishing

A Published Author and Industry Voice

Daniel J. Cullen is a published author whose writing draws on his dual life as an industry operator and community contributor. His work examines the intersection of skilled trades, workforce culture, and the kind of leadership that produces lasting results rather than short-term wins. Whether writing about metal fabrication trends or the character it takes to lead a team, his voice is clear, practical, and grounded in real experience.

His perspectives on Wisconsin's manufacturing workforce challenges reflect the same no-nonsense clarity he brings to his daily work at Precision Metal Fab. Beyond his formal writing, Daniel is active across a range of online communities and platforms — follow him on X (formerly Twitter) for regular commentary on leadership, industry, and Wisconsin life.

Family & Personal Life

Husband, Father, and Lifelong Learner

At the center of Daniel Cullen's life is his family. He is a devoted husband to Haley Cullen — founder of Haley Cullen Wellness — and the proud father of four children, all of whom are growing up in the Greater Waukesha area. The same values he models at work — discipline, accountability, and genuine investment in others — show up every day at home.

Outside of work and family, Daniel stays physically active through boxing and outdoor training, and finds meaning in the slower rhythms of faith and parish life at St. Anthony's on the Lake. He believes that a full life is built not from a single role but from all of them — professional, civic, athletic, and spiritual — working together.
